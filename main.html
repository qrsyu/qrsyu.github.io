Qiaorong Shannon Yu

Hi, I'm Shannon. I am a Ph.D. student in Heeger Lab and Winawer Lab at New York University. I am interested in the computational cognitive neuroscience of human internally generated sensory experience, dreaming and consciousness.

My current research focues on how internally generated activity patterns drift over time and scale with working memory load, and how these dynamics can account for characteristic behavioral error patterns observed in human working memory. Using computational models, I study how biologically intrinsic noise interacts with recurrent neural dynamics to shape the stability and variability of these internal representations, linking circuit-level processes to behavioral measures of memory performance.

I have completed my Master's degree in mathematical and theoretical physics at University of Oxford. I worked with Prof Vijay Balasubramanian on understanding the principles of hippocampal spatial and temporal coding using computational modeling.
